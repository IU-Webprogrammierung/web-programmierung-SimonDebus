DIY plant container app - sustainability meets creativity

The fictional app “ReFurnish” combines upcycling with urban gardening and offers simple step-by-step instructions, to transform old furniture into stylish plant containers.

Users can find instructions in the DIY area by furniture categories, share their own projects in the community feed and get inspired or pass on usable furniture free of charge in the ad market.

The intuitive design and sustainable vision make the app the ideal platform for creative DIY enthusiasts and plant lovers

  • Competitor and target group analysis
  • Wireframing & prototyping
  • Information architecture & usability
  • Interaction design & testing

Interactive mirror for a seamless shopping experience

In this project, I developed a concept for an interactive mirror for use in stationary retail.

Among other things, the mirror allows customers to search for products directly on the display, check availability in the store in real time and view their exact location in the store.

This makes the shopping experience more intuitive and efficient by integrating digital functions directly into the physical sales area. These can be implemented both at the entrance to the store and in changing booths.

  • Competitor and target group analysis
  • Wireframing & prototyping
  • Information architecture & usability
  • Interactive touchscreen design
